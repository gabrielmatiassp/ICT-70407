"FISHING"


What is FISHING? Email phishing is a cyber attack where fraudsters send fake emails that appear to be from legitimate sources to trick users into clicking on malicious links or entering personal information. To protect against phishing attacks, users should be wary of unsolicited emails, avoid clicking on links or attachments from unknown sources, and use security software to detect and block phishing attempts.


How can I protect me? To protect yourself, it's important to carefully verify the senders of emails, never click on suspicious links or download attachments, and avoid providing sensitive information in response to unsolicited emails. Use anti-virus and anti-phishing software and keep your operating system and software up-to-date to protect against known vulnerabilities. Additionally, educate yourself about phishing tactics and stay up-to-date on the latest cyber threats to ensure you're always prepared to protect yourself.

    Cybersecurity

    Cybersecurity, also known as computer security, is the set of practices, measures, and technologies adopted to protect computer systems, networks, electronic devices, and data against digital threats such as cyber attacks, unauthorized access, data theft, and damage to information technology infrastructure. The goal of cybersecurity is to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information and digital resources, as well as to prevent and mitigate potential damages caused by digital threats.



    Why is cybersecurity important

    Cybersecurity is important because it protects sensitive information, prevents cyber attacks, preserves trust and reputation, ensures continuity of operations, ensures compliance with regulations, and protects against financial losses. It plays a crucial role in safeguarding critical infrastructure and creating a secure digital environment for individuals and organizations.

    Data Breach

    A data breach is a security incident in which information is accessed without authorization. Data breaches can hurt businesses and consumers in a variety of ways. They are a costly expense that can damage lives and reputations and take time to repair.

    As technology progresses, more and more of our information has been moving to the digital world. As a result, cyberattacks have become increasingly common and costly. We’ve created a 7-step checklist to use in the case of a data breach. Depending on the severity of the breach and the information exposed, you’ll need to apply some or all of these steps to varying degree. Seven Step Checklist

    • Confirm the breach if your information was exposed
    • Find out what type of data was stolen
    • Accept the breached company's offer(s) to help
    • strengthen your online logins, passwords and security
    • Contact the right people and take additional action

    Confirm the breach and if your information was exposed

    1. Confirm the breach and if your information was exposed. The first step is to confirm that a breach actually occurred. When a data breach occurs, scammers may reach out to you posing as the breached company to try to obtain more of your personal information. Don't fall for fake emails. Go to the company's secure website and/or call the company to confirm the breach and whether your information was involved.

    Find out what type of data was stolen

    2. Find out what type of data was stolen. Why does the type of information exposed matter? While stolen credit cards and the like can be cancelled and replaced, it's quite difficult to obtain a new Social Security number. And fraudsters can do a lot more with your SSN and other unique, sensitive PII than they can accomplish with an email or credit card account.
